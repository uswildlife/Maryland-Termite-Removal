Pikesville MD

Pikesville, in Baltimore County, Maryland , United States, is a census-designated place (CDP). Pikesville lies just northwest of the city limits of Baltimore. It is the closest suburb to Baltimore in the northwest.

At the 2010 census , the population was 30,764. One of the larger Jewish populations in Maryland is in the corridor along Interstate 795, which links Pikesville, Owings Mills and Reisterstown to the Baltimore Beltway (Interstate 695).

History Of Pikesville

Pikesville was named after Zebulon Pike, an American soldier and explorer. “Although in many other states there are places named for Pike, Pikesville, Maryland, is the only contemporary place named” Pikesville “(compare Pikeville, Kentucky).

The (historic) city of Pikesville, incorporated in Barren County , Kentucky, in August 1818, was apparently also named for Zebulon Pike. A new postal name, “Flippin, Kentucky,” was adopted by the community in 1858 to avoid confusion with Pikeville, Kentucky. Indian Creek’s “Pikesville Branch” is the only remaining physical landmark of Kentucky ‘s historic Pikesville, along with a colorful story of an almost forgotten past.

Education

Several elementary, middle, high schools and higher education facilities serve Pikesville. The two higher education facilities in Pikesville in Ner Israel Rabbinical College and Maalot Baltimore: Women’s Institute of Torah Seminary.

Transportation

With two stops in the area, the Baltimore Metro Subway runs through Pikesville, both named for the roads on which they are located: Milford Mill and Old Court.

On Reisterstown Road, 83 and 37 on Old Court Road, 85 on Milford Mill Road/Slade Avenue, and 34 and on Smith Avenue, Maryland Transit Administration bus routes serving Pikesville include Nos. 83 and 89.

Notable Residents Of Pikesville

  • Michael H. Shamberg, filmmaker and music video producer (True Faith, Blue Monday)
  • Robin Quivers, long-running news anchor and co-host of The Howard Stern Show, was born and raised in Pikesville
  • Sam Barsky, knitting artist
