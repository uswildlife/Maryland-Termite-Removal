Termites

What Are Termites?

Termites are regarded as one of the world’s most destructive pests. Termites are insects, most of which feed on dead plant matter, such as woodchips, logs, leaf litter, and soil. They are sometimes referred to as “white ants” but they are only partially related to ants.

Termites Native To Maryland

Eastern Subterranean Termites are a severe issue in Maryland. The average cost of termite-related damage in the US is more than $5 billion per year. Termites cause more damage to homes in the United States than fires, floods , storms and severe winds combined.

Termite colonies vary from three million termites to six million each. Underground tunnels extending up to 100 yards from the main colony can be created by each termite colony. Termites have been around for millions of years, but it has only been in the past 60 years that termite damage has posed a major threat to homes.

Nowadays, whether you are constructing, buying, or selling a home, you should have a Maryland termite inspection. There are several termite exterminators in Maryland that will provide a thorough termite inspection and assist you with Maryland termite prevention.

The Colony Types of Termites

Termite the Queen

Even highly skilled termite exterminators rarely see the Queen. This makes it hard for a termite colony to get rid of. The queen termite lives for 45 years and she produces almost 2,000 eggs a day as she mates for life.

With the potential day after day of creating 2,000 new termites, this is truly a staggering number. The overcrowding, when you think about it, is incredible! In Maryland or Northern Virginia, this is why it is always good to be proactive about your termite removal!

Worker Termites

Worker termites are white and generally blind in color. To permit proper growth, they move the eggs around the colony. It is the task of these worker termites to digest cellulose and feed the colony. The workers make up around 60 percent of the colony and can be found in decaying, wet wood.

Termites Soldier

The worker termites are often guarded by fierce, combative soldier termites, carrying the food and water back to the base camp. Soldier termites are blind and, since the jaws of the soldiers are too large and prohibit feeding, they often have to be fed by the workers. The termite’s primary enemy is the ant. However, the ants are no match for the soldiers.

Habitat Of Maryland’s Termites

In the soil, subterranean termites construct their colonies and build pathways in the soil that are used to locate wood sources that remain below ground or on the surface of the soil. Wood located above ground, such as a house’s wooden construction components, is reached by termite workers moving through earthen tunnels called mud tubes connecting wood to the soil above ground.

Termite workers (the termite group that builds mud tubes) consume cellulose and feed other members of the colony, are very susceptible to dehydration when not in a moist, underground environment, so they die quickly and the colony suffers if they are not in a moist habitat.

If you suspect subterranean termites are having issues, look for their mud tubes. Much of the time, however, mud tubes can be hidden and difficult to locate by homeowners, one of the reasons why subterranean termite species in the United States accounts for the majority of termite damage caused each year.

What do Terms Eat?

Cellulose, the most abundant organic compound found in nature, is sought out by termites. It is the primary building block of plants and is found every day in many materials that humans use. To get the cellulose they need, termites feed on many of these materials: plants, plant by-products, cotton fibers , paper products and, of course, wood.

To derive the cellulose and nutrients that they need to live, termites eat wood. In their gut, termites have protozoa and bacteria that allow them to break down cellulose fibers in wood, which are difficult to digest for other creatures. These organisms transform these cellulose fibers into a nutritious meal and provide access for termites to a food source that is largely ignored by other species.

Subterranean termites like to consume the soft springwood fibers and leave behind the harder summerwood. Wood eaten by subterranean termites resembles a honeycomb, and dirt and fecal particles are present in many of its galleries.

Signs Of Termites Damage

Until infestations are full-blown, interior damage may not become apparent. Termite damage to the home sometimes appears similar to water damage. Buckling wood, swollen floors and ceilings, areas that appear to be suffering from slight water damage and visible mazes within walls or furniture are outward signs of termite damage. Similar to mildew or mold, termite infestations can also exude a scent.

Through mud tunnels that they produce from saliva, mud and feces, subterranean termites also access above-ground food sources. Near the base of infested homes, these tunnels are located.

Termite Removal & Prevention

The best chance at nipping your infestation in the butt is contacting professional termite experts. They are trained to find exactly where colonies are based, to identify the queen, and even to invite colonization in the intricacies of construction. Among pest professionals, there are several distinct termite treatment methods used regularly.

Bait Systems Bait Systems

Bait systems are designed to lure termites with a removal agent that is intended to kill off the colony away from your property. The bait stations are placed around your property and worker termites who think it is food carry the agent back to the nest. When a moderate termite infestation has already been detected in the home, this method is often used.

Sprays with Liquid and Foam

Liquid and/or foam applications often make the most sense from a preventive standpoint. An exterminator treats a liquid or foaming removal agent on the perimeter of your home that kills termites on contact, preventing them from ever entering your property in the first place. It is particularly effective at controlling infestations of subterranean termites.

Fumigation

Fumigation (or ‘tenting’) may be necessary in cases of severe termite infestation, especially where drywood termites are involved, in order to completely rid your home of pests. Exterminators may need to tent your entire property and then use a fumigant that penetrates all wood surfaces in the home, killing all pests, since termites can live in furniture , walls, and even your attic. These treatments are designed specifically to leave no residue behind, but they are often the most uncomfortable.
