Termite Defense You Can Count On!

Termites can be small, but big problems can be caused. In reality, termites cause over $5 billion in property damage every single year in the United States alone. That’s a great deal of cash and a lot of damage! Unfortunately, the reason termites can be so damaging is that they can remain undetected for months or even years once they enter, allowing them to eat away at the wooden structures within your home and cause extensive and expensive harm.

Rarely do these harmful pests come above ground, so they can enter entirely unnoticed homes. We can help, if you already have an active infestation on your property or just want to take preventive measures against termites!

What Are Termites?

Termites are insects living in colonies that feed on dead material and cellulose from plants. You can find three main types of termites throughout the region. Your Baltimore home might be vulnerable to any one of the three. Subterranean, dry wood, and damp wood termites are all potentially harmful to your home, but, among other features, they are differentiated by their dietary and locational preferences:

  • Subterranean termites– In order to survive, these pests must be near the ground or a secondary source of moisture. In the spring or summer, they usually swarm, most frequently after heavy rain. During their swarming process, subterranean termites are usually small and black.
  • Dry wood termites-In areas without a secondary source of moisture, if you spot termites, they are almost definitely dry wood termites, which can live their entire lives inside a structural timber or furniture item. At night, these bugs like to swarm and are typically larger than their subterranean counterparts.
    There are many types of damp wood termites, all of which are much larger than subterranean termites.
  • Damp wood termites. They have big heads and are drawn to wood that is destroyed by water.

How To Keep Out Termites

Cut off their food and water: store firewood more than 20ft away from the house. Maintain a line of 6 to 12 inches between mulch/soil and parts of the building’s wood; leaves should be at least 3 feet away. Aim sprinklers away from the building’s foundation, and direct downspouts away from the house.

Take precautions: do not bring home lumber unless most new boards have been treated to turn off termites. Keep vents clear to circulate dry air. Regularly air out the attics, basements, and crawl spaces.

Go on the defensive: fill or fix any points of entry, from torn siding to cracks in the concrete of your basement. Check for signs of termite activity by monitoring porches, fence posts and sill plates.

Termite Damage To Your Home

Termites can damage your house’s structural wood, as well as the areas around your windows and doors. Serious infestations can even lead to damage to your home ‘s walls and the inside of the wood furniture. This damage potential makes a termite infestation, one that you can not allow to go untreated.

Damage to the structural wood of your building

In order to survive, certain types of termites need to be near moisture. The subterranean termite is one instance of this. Subterranean termites usually stay close to soil rich in moisture and even construct their colonies in the soil. This implies that termite issues typically begin in areas such as the crawl space or along the foundation walls with the foundational wood of the house. It can be costly to damage these areas because it is difficult to repair and often affects other areas of your house.

Damage to the wood around your doors and windows

There are many areas where there is wood in your house that you can not inspect easily. This involves the wood beneath your house, the wood inside your walls, and the attic inside. You will have to be willing to make the time to crawl under the house or climb into the attic if you want to keep an eye on these areas for termite damage. There are other areas made of wood around your home that you can easily check as often as you like for termite damage. The wood around your windows and doors is included in these areas. In order to install doors and windows in houses, builders usually use wood frames. Wood that looks wavy, has voids throughout it, or is weak are signs of termite damage.

Wood damage on the inside of your house

If they are not eradicated, termites that start out on the wood beneath your house will eventually move inside your walls. In the wood furniture and other elements inside your home, there is even a type of termite that will eat away. This termite type is called a ‘dry wood termite’ and the contact with moisture that is characteristic of subterranean termites is not required.
